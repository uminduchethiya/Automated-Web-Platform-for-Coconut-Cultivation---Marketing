Coconut Verities and Forms


Coconut varities and forms

  • Endemic coconut genotypes have been grouped in to 3 distinct varieties (based on (1) morphology and (2) breeding habits)

  • Each variety has several forms

    Variety Description
    Tall Typica Grown on commercial scale
    Dwarf Nana Grown for breeding & ornamental purposes
    King coconut Aurantiaca Grown for drinking purpose (tender nut water )

    Dwarf variety (Nana)

  • In 1960’s 2 improved cultivars produced and released by CRI namely:

    • CRIC 60 (Tall x Tall) (T x T)

    • CRIC 65 (Dwarf x Tall) (D x T)


CRIC – 60 (T x T)

  • Wide ecological adaptability

  • Potential production: 100 nuts/palm /year

  • Widely promoted for commercial cultivation

  • Suitable for all coconut growing areas

CRIC – 65 (Dwarf x Tall) (D x T)

  • Potential production: 120 nuts/palm/year

  • Sensitive to soil moisture stress (during dry season)

  • Not suitable for all coconut growing districts

  • Potential area: 20% of coconut growing area

  • Promoted for cultivation in home gardens

  • Not suitable for commercial cultivation of coconut production (for copra industry)

  • Proven ability to get high toddy yield (due to higher number of inflorescence produced by DT hybrids)

  • Toddy yields:
    • DxT verities: 1250 ml/palm/day

    • Tall verities: 700 ml/palm/day

  • In 1998, CRI produced a new hybrid namely CRISL – 98(Tall x San Ramon)

CRISL – 98 (Tall x San Ramon)

  • Large nuts

  • High copra out turn (300 – 400 g/nut)

  • High quality copra

  • Therefore, high potential for to be used in copra industry

  • Suitable for drought affected areas

  • Flowers 8 – 10 months earlier than T x T verities



National Replanting and New planting programme

  • Annual replanting target: 2% of area

Annual seed nut requirement:

  • Coconut Seed gardens (of CRIC – 60 and CRIC – 65) provide 30 – 40% of requirement

  • The rest amount of required coconut seed nuts is selected from "Superior Tall Palms" (Plus palms)

Plus palms

  • Selected from high yielding blocks of well managed states (after considering yield figures for past 5 years)

  • Produce more than 60 nuts/palm/year

  • Husked weight 680 g

  • Age: 15 – 45 years

  • Nearly 12 – 15% yield improvement can be obtained from seedlings received from plus palms
